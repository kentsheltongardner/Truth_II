Bosses
Overview

Bosses in Truth II are typically enemies that CT-41 only encounters once, and that act as impediments to CT-41's progress. Many Bosses have pages dedicated to them in the Encyclopedia. This page contains more complete descriptions of the Bosses, their fighting styles, strengths and weaknesses, and the battle arenas in which they live or operate.

Bronze Executor

The Bronze Executor is the smallest of the three Executors CT-41 encounters during its journeys, and is in a state of considerable disrepair. The Bronze Executor confronts CT-41 in the antechamber that leads to the Ancestral Halls. Some of its armor panels are missing, and the remains of several Servants in the antechamber suggests that the Bronze Executor has been weakened by battle.

When the Bronze Executor notices CT-41 and powers up to attack, it does so slowly and jerkily. Sparks and exposed wiring show the Executor's vulnerabilities, and its weaponry and shields activate inconsistently. When it becomes clear to the Executor that it cannot defeat CT-41, it activates its self-destruct device and moves toward CT-41. Due to its slow speed, CT-41 is able to maneuver away from the suicidal contraption. When the Bronze Executor detonates, it creates a pressure wave that knocks CT-41 away from it, and also blows the doors off the locked antechamber.

Watcher

The Watcher is a small drone, created by the Outsider to observe goings-on in the Underground. It deals no damage, but does carry a flashlight, measuring lights, and an optical burst untended to temporarily blind creatures that draw too near. The Watcher is incredibly fast and nimble, and highly difficult to corner and destroy. Certain Watchers carry extra sensory equipment, which CT-41 can take if it destroys them.

Once CT-41 has these sensors, its HUD will indicate anomalous readings.
