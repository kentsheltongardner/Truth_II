Fragment
Overview

A Fragment of Thosmodeme was a portion of himself, deliberately sundered from him to serve his purposes. Each of his Fragments was a potent Singularity, possessing great power over Selves in its vicinity.

During his reign as Lord of Lumen, Thosmodeme produced two types of Fragments. A Greater Fragment was a Fragment powerful enough to take on a physical form of its own using surrounding materials. A Lesser Fragment, though still formidable, needed to be housed within a living creature, called a Vessel.

Thosmodeme feared that if his Fragments were separated from him for too long, their wills might begin to diverge from his. As such, they were constantly monitored and dominated by him, and were not permitted to create Fragments of their own. All Fragments returned periodically to the Throneroom, to have their wills realigned with Thosmodeme's.

Due to his use of Fragments and his resulting diminishment, Thosmodeme was less powerful than Voronash during the battle in the Throneroom.

Greater Fragment

Greater Fragments were Thosmodeme's most capable and fearsome creations. They were large portions of his will, sundered from him to carry his purposes abroad. Although the Greater Fragments could assume physical form, it took them significant effort to maintain, and as such they spent much of their lives in an ethereal existence.

Thosmodeme only produced three Greater Fragments during his reign. These were Fesh'togar, the Spirit of Air, Uruthmu, the Spirit of Water, and Azeselon, the Spirit of Fire. Human Children under Thosmodeme's sway in the Sanctuary referred to these Fragments as Fesh'togar-Thosmodeme, Uruthmu-Thosmodeme, and Azeselon-Thosmodeme, reflecting their oneness with their maker.

The three Greater Fragments were birthed in response to the Native Uprising, as Natives protected by the Secret Thought pushed into the Temple. The Greater Fragments were Thosmodeme's destroying angels, tasked with the annihilation of the rebels. Together with hordes of Servants the Greater Fragments pushed the Natives back.

After the Native rebellion was routed and dispersed in the Assembly Hall, Fesh-Togar was sent to the Shallows, Uruthmu to the Womb, and Azeselon to the Deep. Obedient to Thosmodeme, they killed and consumed most Natives they encountered, sparing only the Deliverer and a few others, and taking them prisoner.

When the Slaughter was complete, Thosmodeme filled the Three with new purpose. Fesh-Togar would interrogate the Deliverer, attempting to break his will and discover a way to thwart his Secret Thought. Uruthmu would capture and uplift the lower creatures of Lumen 2, raising them to be worthy Servants to Thosmodeme. Azeselon would attempt to Synchronize with the Magma Worms, whose young dwelt in the Magma Chambers. During the course of their missions, the Three returned to the Throneroom several times every Turn, to make a complete report of their activities, and to have their wills realigned with their Thosmodeme's.

Like Thosmodeme, each Fragment possessed a Domain, the area in which they were able to project their will. Within that Domain was a Sanctum, an area Children and Servants were forbidden to enter unless explicitly bidden. Within their Domains were living doors, called Sentinels, which only opened when they sensed the presence of their master.

Fesh'togar

Fesh'togar, or the Spirit of Air, was a Greater Fragment of Thosmodeme, originally created along with his brothers to quell the Native Uprising. After the Uprising was crushed and the rebelling Natives were exterminated or captured, Thosmodeme sent Fesh'togar to the Healers' Locus. There he was tasked with breaking the mind and will of Native captives, including the Deliverer. Fesh'togar ultimately hoped to force the captured Natives to reproduce once their minds were his.

Though Fesh'toger failed to defeat the Secret Thought, he did discover ways to animate the bodies of the dead. After destroying a Native Seed he could still control its limbs and vital functions, producing beings such as the Puppet from Native corpses. He tried, and failed, to produce new Natives from Seeds and tissues kept in medical storage. Fesh'togar's assumed form was ghastly and ethereal, reflecting his association with the dead, and reminiscent of the howling, unpredictable winds in his Sanctum.

Uruthmu

Uruthmu, or the Spirit of Water, was a Greater Fragment of Thosmodeme, originally created with his two brothers to destroy the rebelling Natives during the Uprising. After the Natives were killed or captured, Uruthmu was sent to the Womb. In its primordial pools, where the Lumene Natives and many other lifeforms first evolved, Uruthmu bred Thosmodeme's Servants. Each existed for a specific purpose, whether that was relaying Thosmodeme's thought, tunneling, destroying threats, or serving as high calorie food. Uruthmu endeavored to raise beings to rationality, but due to his overriding control, his subjects never had sufficient autonomy to develop reason.

After settling into the Womb, Uruthmu took on a form that allowed him to manipulate and move quickly through the waters of his Domain.

Azeselon

Azeselon, or the Spirit of Fire, was a Greater Fragment of Thosmodeme, originally created along with his brothers to quash the Native Uprising. After the Slaughter, Azeselon was sent by Thosmodeme to the caverns nearest the Magma Chambers. There he discovered new Servants to obey Thosmodme, and attempted to communicate with the Magma Worm young.

Azeselon's Domain covered portions of the Magma Chambers and the Engineers' Locus. In his fiery abode Azeselon took on his lasting form, one that allowed him to exist in evironments of extreme heat. He was a being of strange armor and fire.

Lesser Fragments and Vessels

Lesser Fragments were Fragments of Thosmodeme too weak to create their own form. Instead they took residence in the bodies of living creatures, called 'Vessels'. Otis Cobb became a Vessel to a Lesser Fragment when Thosmodeme brought him to the Throneroom. When the Vessels that bore Lesser Fragments grew old or feeble, or when Thosmodeme decided their purpose was fulfilled, he called them back to himself to be resorbed.

As part of his own Purification, Thosmodeme would occasionally cast out portions of himself that he considered chaotic or destabilizing. These Lesser Fragments would be vomited forth into expendable Servants, called 'Vessels of Filth'. These unpredictable Vessels were then taken by Temple Guards to the Pit, to be consumed by Voronash. Voronash became increasingly volatile as he was fed Vessels of Filth for an Age.

Truth II

During the events of Truth II, CT-41 encounters and defeats the Greater Fragments of Thosmodeme, and captures them in the Necrolith. Each Greater Fragment that CT-41 captures allows it to pass by its corresponding Sentinels. CT-41 also defeats and captures several Lesser Fragments in the Temple, including the Temple Guards. Capturing any of these Fragments greatly increases the power available to the Commentary and the Native Seeds that accompany CT-41.
