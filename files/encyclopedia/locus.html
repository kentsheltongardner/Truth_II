Locus
Overview

A Locus was a geographical region wherein Natives of a particular Lineage gathered and pursued their joint interests. The Native Loci centered around the Womb and the Sea, and together comprised the Realm.

Members of a Lineage typically built their homes and facilities within the borders of their Locus. The boundaries of the Loci were delineated and updated during Great Councils, with disputed areas deemed off-limits to parties contesting ownership until a resolution was reached.

All Native Lineages other than the Explorers and the Laborers had an officially recognized Locus. Before the Destruction the Explorers had no permanent home, and spent much of their time in solitary exploration. The Laborers were initially dispersed throughout the Realm to serve the other Lineages in realizing their diverse pursuits.

Though the Laborers had no official Locus, they did congregate to certain areas of the Underground near the Farmland, and the Refuge especially. During the final Turn of the Hidden Age, and under the influence of Presence, the Laborers began constructing and excavating their own Locus beneath the Refuge. At the center of this pseudo-Locus was the Temple of Thosmodeme.

After the Destruction, the Explorers were constrained by the Rulers' mandate to live in the Badlands. The undesirable region was not a true Locus, but by most practical measures it served as such.

Locus Door

Within a typical Locus were doors that only responded to commands from members of the corresponding Lineage. These doors were visibly distinct from regular doors, and were typically the same color as the Lineage's tribal color.

During the events of Truth II, CT-41 is able to pass through new Locus Doors as it is joined by the empowered Seeds of various Natives.
