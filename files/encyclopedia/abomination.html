Abomination
Overview

In the religion of Purification taught and enforced by Thosmodeme, 'Abomination' was the epithet given to all artificial constructs acting in apparent imitation of natural life.

Thosmodeme found highly rational, naturally occurring beings to be the most interesting offspring of Creation. These were the least obviously deterministic systems he encountered, and the ones he deemed most worthy of his guidance. Contrariwise, Thosmodeme found artificial lifeforms uninteresting, yet difficult or impossible to manipulate, due to the blunt and inflexible nature of the logical systems that controlled them. As such, Thosmodeme feared Destroyer constructs, Native robots and Executors, and CT-41, and dubbed them 'Abomination'.

The Servants of Thosmodeme and his Fragments destroyed Abominations wherever they found them, and if it was impractical to destroy them, they avoided them completely.

Truth II

During the events of Truth II, many of the inhabitants of the Sanctuary refer to CT-41 as 'Abomination'.
