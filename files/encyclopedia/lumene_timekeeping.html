Lumene Timekeeping
Overview

Several calendars were used by the various Lineages of Kindred. The most common units of calendar time were the Turn, the Life, and the Cycle.

Bathing

A Bathing began as a colloquial unit of time, roughly equal to the amount of time a Lumene Native could spend in atmosphere, before having to return to the water for hydration. In the Enlightened Age, a Bathing was standardized as a fraction of a Turn.

Turn

The Turn was a unit of measurement, proposed by Explorers at the inagural Great Council. The Turn corresponded to one Lumene day, or 56 Earth years. Lumene Natives considered the Turn to begin at noontime, when Lumen was directly above the Fortuna Crater. The modern Lumene Calendar began at Turn zero, or the first Lumene noon after the Unification. Dates before that time were expressed as negative numbers, and dates afterward as positives.

Life

The Life was an older unit for measuring time, equal to the length of a Lumene Native's natural lifespan. The Life corresponded to roughly 40,000 Earth years. All Lumene Natives were familiar with this unit of time from birth, having inherited it in the Chain of Memory. Although the Turn was universally accepted as the standard unit of measure after the Unification, the Life was still used informally to refer to long periods of time.

Cycle

The Cycle was a unit of time used by Lineages that dwelt near the Upper Mantle, such as the Faithful and the Worshippers. A new Cycle was understood to begin at the time that Magma Worms deposited new young into the Magma Chambers beneath the Fortuna Crater. These birthings were accompanied by regular, noticeable seismic activity. The Cycle was equal to approximately 40,000 Earth years.
