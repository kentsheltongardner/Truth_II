Old God
Overview

The Old Gods were the most intelligent of the Ancients, and were the apex predators of the Lumene Underground from the late Aquatic Age until the Pre-Tribal Extinction. These monstrous creatures bored enormous tunnels through the crust while searching for prey. These tunnels were used by many other creatures of the Underground, including Lumene Natives, and many were still traveled well into the later Ages of Lumen 2.

When an enormous coronal mass ejection that ended the Emergent Age caused nearly all life on the surface of Lumen 2 to go extinct, the Old Gods entered Dormancy, waiting for the return of their prey. In the thousands of Turns that followed, Lumene Natives took their place atop the predatory hierarchy of the Underground.

Hunting

The Old Gods developed a symbiotic relationship with Proto-Natives, long before the advent of the Common Ancestor and the Luminary. When a Proto-Native spotted a large prey animal that it could not overpower, it would let out howls that echoed through the Underground. If one of the Old Gods heard the Native's howls, it would follow them toward its prey. The Native would pursue its frightened and desperate quarry, maintaining distance and howling until the Old God rushed in, slaying the beast and consuming its flesh.

If it was pleased by the meal, and the Native reverenced it, the Old God would leave much of the carcass for the Native to scavenge. However, if an Old God encountered a Native without an offering, it did not hesitate to eat the Native or its offspring.

Legacy

A deep feeling of awe, terror and reverence developed in the mind of the Simpleton toward the Old Gods, a feeling which persisted as long as the Chain of Memory. Thosmodeme, at the start of the Subjugation, entered the minds of many Laborers as an Old God, and was thus able to command their obedience.

Lumene Natives of the High Lineages bore a dark memory of their final encounter with an Old God, which they called the 'Shame'.

Old Gods

During the events of Truth II, CT-41 encounters and awakens four of the Old Gods from their Dormancy, and finds one Old God that was killed during the Destruction. After finding, rousing and defeating the enormous creatures, it is able to use materials taken from their bodies to create incredibly powerful items.

When CT-41 enters the chamber of an Old God, its Companions do not follow.

Ashtaroth

CT-41 finds the Old God Ashtaroth overgrown by Deep Roots near the Enclave of the Faithful. CT-41 learns from the Groundskeeper that the Faithful knew of the Old God's presence, and chose to keep it hidden and undisturbed.
